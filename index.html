How to Play Chess?


Chess is a popular board game that's easy to learn but takes a lifetime to master! Here's a basic things that you have to know:




Objective & Setups

Objective: The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king. This means putting their king in a position where it's under attack and cannot escape capture.


Setup: Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The board has 64 squares in an 8x8 grid.




Piece Movement

1: King: Can move one square in any direction.

2: Queen: Moves diagonally, horizontally, or vertically any number of squares available.

3: Rook: Moves horizontally or vertically any number of squares available.

4: Bishop: Moves diagonally in any number of squares available.

5: Knight: Moves in an L-shape (two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular).

6: Pawn: Moves forward one square (two squares on its first move) but captures diagonally.




Gameplay

1. Players take turns to make one move at a time.

2. When a piece is moved onto a square occupied by an opponent's piece, it captures that piece.

3.If a king is under attack, the player must make a move to get the king out of danger:

  • This can involve moving the king to a safe square.
  • Capturing the threatening piece.
  • Blocking the attack by placing another piece in between.
  • If a player puts their opponent's king in a position where it cannot escape capture, it's called checkmate, and that player wins the game.
  • If a player puts their opponent's king in a position where it cannot escape capture but not in check or not under attacked, it's called stalemate,it considered to be as draw and neither wins the game.



Special Moves

1: Castling - King moves two squares towards a rook and the rook moves to the square next to the king. (Make sure that your king path to the rook is clear before you castle)

2: En Passant -A special pawn capture that can occur when a pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position and lands next to an opponent's pawn, allowing that opponent to capture it "as if" it had only moved one square forward. (only legal if white pawn reaches in fifth rank and black reaches fourth rank and capture the pawn)

Preview:

3: Pawn promotion -When a pawn reaches the opponent's back rank, it can be promoted to any other piece (except a king).





That's it i hope you learned the basics of playing chess and apply it to the chessboard! Happy playing!
